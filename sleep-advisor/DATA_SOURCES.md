# Data Sources & Academic Citations

Lumina Sleep Advisor incorporates insights from the following publicly available datasets, peer-reviewed publications, and research resources. All data is used for educational and informational purposes only.

---

## 1. National Sleep Foundation

**National Sleep Foundation.** "Sleep Facts & Statistics."  
🔗 [sleepfoundation.org/how-sleep-works/sleep-facts-statistics](https://www.sleepfoundation.org/how-sleep-works/sleep-facts-statistics)

Used for general sleep hygiene recommendations, statistical facts, and evidence-based sleep guidelines.

---

## 2. Kaggle — Sleep Health and Lifestyle Dataset

**Laksika Tharmalingam (2023).** "Sleep Health and Lifestyle Dataset." *Kaggle.*  
🔗 [kaggle.com/datasets/uom190346a/sleep-health-and-lifestyle-dataset](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/uom190346a/sleep-health-and-lifestyle-dataset)

Used for correlations between stress, physical activity, BMI, and sleep quality.

---

## 3. National Sleep Research Resource (NSRR)

**National Sleep Research Resource.** Funded by the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute (NHLBI). De-identified physiological signals and clinical data from well-characterized research cohorts and clinical trials.  
🔗 [sleepdata.org](https://sleepdata.org/datasets)

### 3a. Sleep Heart Health Study (SHHS)

**Quan, S.F. et al. (1997).** "The Sleep Heart Health Study: design, rationale, and methods." *Sleep*, 20(12), 1077–1085.  
Multi-center cohort study, n=6,441 men and women aged 40+.  
🔗 [sleepdata.org/datasets/shhs](https://sleepdata.org/datasets/shhs)

Used for cardiovascular risk associations with sleep-disordered breathing.

### 3b. MrOS — Osteoporotic Fractures in Men Study

**Orwoll, E. et al. (2005).** Osteoporotic Fractures in Men Study. NSRR. 5,994 community-dwelling men aged 65+, 6 clinical centers.  
🔗 [sleepdata.org/datasets/mros](https://sleepdata.org/datasets/mros)

Used for insights on sleep disorders and fracture/mortality risk in elderly men.

### 3c. Cleveland Family Study (CFS)

**Redline, S. et al. (1995).** Cleveland Family Study. NSRR. 2,284 individuals (46% African American) from 361 families.  
🔗 [sleepdata.org/datasets/cfs](https://sleepdata.org/datasets/cfs)

Used for familial aggregation patterns of sleep apnea.

---

## 4. Sleep Research Society

**Sleep Research Society.** Public Datasets & NSRR Actigraphy Resources.  
🔗 [sleepresearchsociety.org](https://sleepresearchsociety.org/career-advancement/public-datasets/)

Used for Sleep Onset Latency and Wake After Sleep Onset benchmark data.

---

## 5. ISRUC-Sleep Dataset

**Khalighi, S., Sousa, T., Santos, J.M. & Nunes, U. (2016).** "ISRUC-Sleep: A comprehensive public dataset for sleep researchers." *Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine*, 124, 180–192.  
🔗 [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/283734463_ISRUC-Sleep_A_comprehensive_public_dataset_for_sleep_researchers)

PSG recordings from the University of Coimbra, scored by two independent experts. Used for sleep architecture disruption insights.

---

## 6. The Human Sleep Project (HSP) v2.0

**Westover, M.B., Moura Junior, V., Thomas, R., Cash, S., Nasiri, S., Sun, H. et al.** The Human Sleep Project. Brain Data Science Platform (BDSP). 26,200+ PSG studies from 19,492 patients, Massachusetts General Hospital.  
🔗 [bdsp.io/content/hsp/2.0](https://bdsp.io/content/hsp/2.0/)  
🔗 [AWS Open Data Registry](https://registry.opendata.aws/bdsp-hsp/)

**How to Cite:** "The Human Sleep Project (HSP) was accessed on [DATE] from https://registry.opendata.aws/bdsp-hsp."

Used for brain aging, neurological risk prediction, and Alzheimer's/cerebrovascular disease findings.

---

## 7. SleepFM — Nature Medicine (2026)

**Thapa, R., Kjaer, M.R., He, B. et al. (2026).** "A multimodal sleep foundation model for disease prediction." *Nature Medicine*, 32, 752–762.  
DOI: [10.1038/s41591-025-04133-4](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41591-025-04133-4)  
🔗 GitHub: [zou-group/sleepfm-clinical](https://github.com/zou-group/sleepfm-clinical)

Trained on 585,000+ hours of PSG recordings from ~65,000 participants. Used for disease prediction insights (130+ conditions from single-night sleep data), including dementia, heart failure, and Parkinson's disease risk.

---

## 8. SleepFounder — medRxiv (2025)

**"A Low-Burden Sleep Foundation Model Built on Respiratory and Heartbeat Signals from 780,000+ Hours of Multi-Ethnic Sleep Recordings."** *medRxiv* (2025).  
🔗 [medRxiv preprint](https://www.medrxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.09.06.25335216v1)

Built from 780,000+ hours of multi-ethnic sleep recordings across 28 cohorts. Used for cardiorespiratory disease prediction insights (heart failure, GERD, cholesterol, sleep apnea prevalence).

---

## Usage & Compliance

- All datasets referenced are **publicly available** or used under their respective open licenses.
- No raw patient data is stored, processed, or transmitted by this application.
- Lumina uses **aggregate statistical findings** and **research conclusions** from these sources to generate educational sleep advice.
- This application is not a medical device and does not provide medical diagnoses.
